About This Tool

What is this?

This is an educational tool that compares different UK pension withdrawal strategies using historical data. It allows you to explore what would have happened if you had withdrawn your pension at various points in history and invested in different asset classes.

The Motivation

For some people, the idea of "dropping out" of the traditional financial system is appealing. Whether due to distrust of banks, concerns about currency debasement, or simply a desire for tangible assets, the question arises: what would it actually cost?

The UK pension system offers a unique opportunity to explore this question. Unlike most countries, UK pension rules (since 2015) allow you to withdraw your entire pension pot at any time after age 55 (rising to 57 from 2028). This flexibility means you genuinely have the choice to:

  • Keep your money in a SIPP (Self-Invested Personal Pension) invested in stocks
  • Withdraw it all, pay the tax, and buy physical gold
  • Or any combination of strategies

The Gold Option

Physical gold has a special status in the UK tax system. Specifically, UK legal tender gold coins (such as Gold Britannias and Gold Sovereigns) are:

  • VAT exempt – no 20% VAT when purchasing
  • CGT exempt – no Capital Gains Tax when selling, regardless of profit

This means if you withdraw your pension (paying income tax on 75% of it), buy gold coins, and they increase in value over time, you pay no additional tax when selling them to fund your retirement.

Compare this to a SIPP where every withdrawal is taxed as income (75% taxable, 25% tax-free).

What This Tool Shows

This tool uses historical data going back to 1980 to show what would have actually happened under each strategy. It compares:

  • Physical Gold – Withdraw pension, pay tax, buy CGT-exempt gold coins
  • Gold ETF SIPP – Keep pension invested in a gold-tracking ETF
  • S&P 500 SIPP – Keep pension invested in US stocks
  • Nasdaq 100 SIPP – Keep pension invested in US tech stocks
  • FTSE 100 SIPP – Keep pension invested in UK stocks
  • US Long Treasury SIPP – Keep pension invested in long-term US bonds (annuity proxy)
  • Combined strategies – 50/50 splits of any two base strategies

Inflation & Purchasing Power

By default, this tool adjusts annual withdrawals for UK inflation (CPI). This means if you specify a 4% withdrawal rate, the actual pound amount you receive will increase each year to match rising prices, ensuring your lifestyle remains consistent throughout your retirement.

This is critical for long-term simulations (20-30 years), as £10,000 in 1990 would need to be roughly £25,000 in 2024 to buy the same amount of goods and services.

All calculations include realistic costs: transaction fees, storage fees, management fees, and accurate historical UK tax rates.

Technical Details

This is an open-source project. All calculations run entirely in your browser – no data is sent to any server.

Source Code: github.com/cainem/bury-it-in-the-garden

Data Sources:

  • Gold prices: LBMA Gold Price (London Bullion Market Association)
  • S&P 500 / Nasdaq 100: S&P Dow Jones and Nasdaq Total Return Indices
  • FTSE 100: FTSE Russell Total Return
  • US Treasuries: Bloomberg/ICE US Treasury 20+ Year Total Return
  • UK Inflation: ONS Consumer Price Index (CPI) historical data
  • Exchange rates: Bank of England and FRED historical data
  • UK tax rates: HMRC historical tax bands and rates

Default Assumptions

Parameter Default Value Notes
Gold transaction cost 3% Covers dealer spread and delivery; realistic for competitive dealers
Gold storage fee 0% per year Default assumes no storage cost; professional vault storage typically costs 0.5%-1.0% per year (adjustable in Advanced Settings). Home storage is not recommended for significant amounts.
SIPP management fee 0.5% per year Typical for low-cost SIPP providers
Pension tax-free portion 25% UK pension rules allow 25% tax-free withdrawal

All fees can be adjusted in the Advanced Settings on the calculator page.
